About Montserrado

Liberia's Most Populated County

Montserrado County is located in the northwestern region of Liberia and is the smallest county by land area but the largest by population. The county hosts Monrovia, the capital city of Liberia, making it the center of national administration and economic activity.

The county is known for its urban development, seaport access, universities, businesses, entertainment centers, beaches, and historical significance in Liberia's modern history.

Montserrado plays a major role in transportation, governance, finance, education, media, and telecommunications within Liberia.

Major Districts & Communities

Montserrado County contains several urban and suburban districts that contribute to Liberia's political and economic activities.

Monrovia

Liberia's capital city and major commercial center known for governance, business, ports, education, and media.

Paynesville

One of Liberia's fastest-growing urban communities with residential, educational, and commercial developments.

Bensonville

The official capital city of Montserrado County with historical and administrative importance.

Bushrod Island

Industrial and port area hosting major shipping, petroleum, and commercial facilities.

New Kru Town

Coastal fishing community known for maritime culture and local seafood activities.

Sinkor

A major urban district with hotels, restaurants, embassies, businesses, and entertainment centers.

Economy & Development

Liberia's Commercial Hub

Montserrado County is Liberia's primary economic center, hosting banks, telecommunications companies, government ministries, universities, shipping activities, and major commercial markets.

The county also supports transportation systems, seaport operations, hospitality services, and digital innovation sectors that contribute to national growth.
